How it works

Three feeds in. One map out.

01 / CAPTURE

Multi-source ingestion

Ground stations stream PM2.5. Citizens upload geotagged photos. Satellites measure aerosol optical depth across regions.

02 / FUSE

Spatial calibration

All inputs align to a single geo-grid. Photo reports and sensor readings reinforce or contradict each other automatically.

03 / PROJECT

24h prediction

Trend models project each cell 24 hours forward, factoring traffic cycles, land use, and recent volatility.

04 / DISPATCH

Automated response

Critical zones auto-alert municipal crews. Mist cannons, cleanup teams, or inspections routed to exact streets.

Capabilities

Built for the blind spots.

Citizen reports

Anyone can flag smoke, dust, or burning in under a minute — geotagged, timestamped, dropped on the grid.

Open-Meteo live feed

Integrated with open atmospheric models. Real-time PM2.5, PM10, and US AQI across all Indian states.

WAQI tile overlay

Global air quality tile layer renders continuous heat contours across the full geography of India.

Hotspot detection

Flags areas with satellite thermal spikes + high citizen reports, identifying zones with no physical sensors.

24h spike forecasting

Rolling forecasts per city — crews can be positioned before the AQI actually crosses into unhealthy.

Dispatch console

Every alert becomes a ticket — acknowledge, assign mist cannon or cleanup crew, track to resolution.
